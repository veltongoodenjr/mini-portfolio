UWI Mona · Instagram & Facebook

Social Media Outcomes Dashboard

A high-level view of how students and prospects responded to UWI Mona’s social media activity between July and October 2025, with a deeper focus on the two months of managed, billed work (September–October).

Context

The original management and billing window began in July–August. Early operational teething troubles created some inconsistency, so the invoiced period was moved forward to September–October, when the system was properly in place.

July’s performance is still included in this report to give an accurate picture of the impact of services rendered once the channels were active at full rhythm, even though it is not part of the final two-month billing block.

Executive summary

Management snapshot

In brief

  • Organic channels are trusted and perform strongly whenever cadence is stable and content is student-centred.
  • Within the final billed period (September–October), Facebook’s cost per follower dropped from about $125 to $2.58 on the same budget.
  • The main risk is cadence dipping during high-interest moments; the main opportunity is locking in the October rhythm across future academic years.

Across the July–October window, students consistently treated UWI Mona’s channels as trusted, official sources of information and campus life. Orientation content, deadline reminders and operational updates all attracted strong engagement whenever they were made visible in the feed.

The initial service period started in July–August. During this time the content structure, approvals and workflows were still settling, which led to some inconsistency in delivery, particularly in August. To keep billing aligned with the value delivered once that structure was properly in place, the formal invoiced period was shifted forward to September–October.

July remains in this report because it shows how the audience behaves when the channels are fully active: over-delivery on posts, very strong engagement and clear demand for timely, useful updates. September then reflects the early ramp-up within the new window, while October shows what happens once cadence and campaigns are operating as intended.

On Facebook, the same monthly budget produced very different outcomes depending on structure. In September, spend was live but inefficient. By October, after tightening targeting and creative, the cost per follower dropped from roughly $125 to $2.58, turning the page into a cost-effective support channel for recruitment and awareness.

The decision to bill for two months (September–October) reflects this reality: early teething troubles are recognised and not charged at full weight, but their data is still used to show the full impact and learning carried into the final billed period.

Top outcome headlines

  • Students reliably engage when content is timely, student-centred and clearly written.
  • The channels are being used as reference points for orientation, deadlines and critical updates.
  • Within the billed period, paid efficiency improved sharply while organic trust remained high.

Top-level next steps

  • Lock in a minimum monthly Instagram cadence tied to the academic calendar.
  • Maintain simple cost-per-follower guardrails on Facebook and act quickly when spend drifts above them.
  • Use this dashboard format as a recurring monthly review tool to guide the 2026 content rhythm.

September – early optimisation

  • Spend was high compared with the number of new followers gained.
  • Audiences were broad, and creative was not tightly aligned with student questions.

October – structured campaigns

  • Audiences focused on current and prospective student profiles.
  • Creative reflected real decisions and concerns at that stage of the semester.
  • CPF dropped sharply while budget remained constant.
Taken together, the two billed months show that Facebook can be an efficient recruitment-support channel when campaigns are tightly defined and reviewed regularly against simple efficiency thresholds, rather than allowed to run on autopilot.

Forward cadence recommendations

Instagram & Facebook

Instagram (organic)

  • Maintain a baseline of 12–16 posts per month in teaching periods.
  • Anchor content to key academic dates: application windows, orientation, exam periods and graduation.
  • Pair each milestone with both a story-driven piece (video) and a clarity-driven piece (carousel).

Facebook (paid)

  • Set a simple CPF target and adjust campaigns that move far above it.
  • Reuse winning themes and creatives from Instagram for consistency.
  • Schedule a short monthly review using this dashboard to keep spend focused on what works.
The teething issues seen in the early months have already informed a more structured approach. Carrying forward the October rhythm and CPF discipline into 2026 will compound gains across future intakes.

Highlights

  • Students reliably engage when content is timely, student-centred and clearly written.
  • The channels have become a go-to reference point for orientation, deadlines and urgent updates.
  • Facebook has demonstrated that, with the right structure, existing budget can deliver efficient audience growth.
  • Lessons from the early months already inform the more disciplined approach seen in the billed period.

Risks & constraints

  • Cadence dips during high-interest periods reduce organic discovery and can make channels feel quiet even when demand for information is high.
  • Without simple CPF targets, paid campaigns can quickly drift into high-cost, low-impact territory.
  • Content operations are sensitive to approval bottlenecks and competing projects, which can impact planned activity.

The approach taken in the billed months already addresses several of these risks. Maintaining that discipline over a full academic year will be more efficient than rebuilding from scratch each semester.

Methodology and how to use this dashboard

Reading guide

Data sources

  • Native analytics from Instagram and Facebook for reach, engagement and growth.
  • Campaign manager data for ad spend and cost-per-follower calculations.
  • Internal content calendar for planned versus actual activity.

Focus on outcomes, not volume alone

  • Numbers are interpreted in terms of what they mean for students: clarity, confidence and guidance.
  • Strong engagement on a smaller number of posts signals demand; it is not a reason to reduce output.
  • Paid efficiency is viewed alongside organic health, not in isolation.

How leadership can use this view

  • Use the executive summary and KPI cards for quick orientation.
  • Use the simple visual bars to understand how activity and efficiency changed over time.
  • Use the recommendations to set expectations and resource levels for the upcoming academic year.

This dashboard is designed to be updated and reused, so that over time UWI Mona can track not only what was posted, but what it achieved for students and for the wider institution.
